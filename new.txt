The first thing I would like to share is how this fraud trend was identified and how the rule was developed.

This rule is for the MBNA portfolio and is designed to identify applications exhibiting characteristics that, based on our analysis, are associated with elevated fraud risk. The analysis focuses on key application attributes including address city, employment income, occupation, and email address.

Before beginning the analysis, I performed data validation to ensure that the data being used was complete, accurate, and suitable for analysis. This included validating the availability and quality of the relevant application fields and confirming that the fraud outcomes were properly captured.

As part of the analysis, I reviewed historical application data over the selected observation period and examined the relationship between address city, employment income, occupation, and email address patterns and confirmed fraud outcomes. The objective was to identify characteristics and combinations of attributes that demonstrated elevated fraud rates while maintaining an acceptable false positive rate.

I then segmented the population across these attributes and evaluated their performance by calculating key metrics such as fraud rate, capture rate, volume, and false positive rate. This allowed me to assess the risk-reward tradeoff and determine whether the identified patterns were sufficiently predictive to be considered for rule implementation.

Based on the results of the analysis, the identified characteristics met the criteria for rule development. At this stage, I translated the analytical findings into a rule construct and implemented it as a test rule within FIQM. Using FIQM's testing capabilities, I evaluated the expected performance and impact of the rule on historical data.

Once the testing results were validated, I completed the required documentation and submitted the rule package for peer review. Following successful peer review, the rule proceeded through the standard governance and implementation process before being considered for production deployment.
